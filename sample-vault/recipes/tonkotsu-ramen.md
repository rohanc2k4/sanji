# Tonkotsu ramen

12-hour pork bone broth. The point is the emulsion — milky, viscous, not greasy.

## Bones

3 lb pork neck bones, 1 lb pig trotter, blanched twice in fresh water before the long simmer. The trotter brings collagen; the neck bones bring fat and flavor.

## Broth

After blanching, cover with fresh cold water, bring to a hard rolling boil, and keep it there for 10–12 hours. Top up with hot water as the level drops. Skim foam in the first hour, then stop fussing — the agitation is what emulsifies the fat into the broth.

## Tare

Shoyu tare is easier than shio. 1:1 soy sauce + sake, simmer with kombu and a single shiitake until reduced by 25%.

## Toppings

Chashu, ajitsuke tamago, scallion greens, optional menma. Skip the corn — that's a Sapporo style.
